About Me

Hello! Again. My name is Ross! I'm from a small town in West Michigan called Montague. As of May 2022, I am employed at Medline Industries in Northfield, Illinois as an Associate Design Engineer. I work in a team with other Electrical, Software, and Mechanical Design Engineers to create designs for new medical device products from scratch. I graduated from the University of Michigan (Go Blue!) in May 2022 with double bachelors degrees in Computer Science and Electrical Engineering.

Growing up in Montague was pretty great! The town is right on Lake Michigan, so I got to spend my formative years on boats and beaches in the summer time. In high school I participated in Football and Track, and was captain of each in my final year. Playing sports in high-school instilled in me a lifelong sense of respect for teamwork and physical exercise. I still try and workout as much as I have time for on weekdays.

My University experience was quite a ride, I went from a fairly small high school class in a rural tourist town to a massive University widely regarded as one of the best in the country. The initial culture shock was pretty intense, but luckily I adjusted and my 5 years at Umich were the best I've had yet. I learned all kinds of incredibly interesting things and met all kinds of interesting people. For a while I didn't really know what I wanted to do with my education. I started as purely an Electrical Engineering major simply because my dad is an Electrician. As I took more courses, I gravitated towards low-level software and software-hardware interactions. Coupled with advice from a few incredible professors, I decided to direct my focus towards Embedded Systems. When the world ground to a halt after COVID-19 hit in 2020, I decided that instead of graduating into a non-existent job market, I would stay one more year and pick up a second major in Computer Science as well, adding a few more specialty classes in my chosen field. Cheaper than Grad School!

My personal hobbies are all over the place. I love trying and experiencing new things. I've done lots of construction, building a porch swing from scratch after the summer of my college freshman year. I've travelled to several beautiful national parks with friends (Grand Teton is my favorite!). I enjoy finding new music and listen to all sorts of different genres and playlists. I've worked on several personal DIY electrical and software projects. My most consistent hobbies are working as mentioned earlier, meditation, and reading.

Lastly I want to touch on two things I'm passionate about. The first of these is Climate Change. It is my belief that Climate Change is the biggest existential threat humanity has faced yet. My ultimate goal is to work at an organization that develops technology to combat Climate Change such as renewable energy or carbon capture and also ties into my own areas of expertise. The second is my two fields of study, I love learning about the math, physics, history, and culture associated with everything in the fields of Electrical Engineering and Computer Science, whether it be electric motors, fourier transforms, databases, serial protocols, integrated circuits, sorting algorithms, or any of the countless other things those fields encompass. I also believe that I am incredibly fortunate to be able to pursue whatever knowledge I choose free of obstruction, something that large parts of the world don't have the luxury of.

Thanks for reading!
